Note: Readers of Tierrabyte have expressed interest in seeing my design process, so for this project, I am sharing the ongoing development including research, wireframing, and prototyping.

Overview


Novel Tea is a virtual platform for readers to host and attend book clubs. It aims to promote a "friends chatting about books by the fireplace" environment by suggesting common snacks, drinks, and a time to discuss via in-app videoconference.

Interviews and User Needs


Over two weeks, I interviewed 18 readers, ranging from casual to avid, to glean insights on how potential users could get the most from a book-centered application.

  • Seven male and 11 female participants
  • Age range: 15 - 61
  • Of the seven male participants, only one said he would participate in a book club.
  • Of the ten participants who said they would be interested in a virtual book club:
    • 100% said they would be interested in attending others' clubs
    • 60% said they would be interested in hosting their own clubs

I also heard from five people, who have not read books in several years, to see they would have any interest in doing so if they had a virtual community to share the experience with.

When I was in high school and we had to read books and discuss them in class, I actually got a lot out of the discussions, so I think there is something to having people to talk about it with.
I like the idea, but I would need something to hold me accountable to read the book before the meeting.

Demographics and Personas


In addition to considering the feedback from the interviews above, I drew demographic information from services that may have similar audiences, including Goodreads and audiobooks. Below are some distilled insights.

  • Both Audiobooks and Goodreads are more popular among women than men.
  • Compared to the US average, Goodreads users are also more likely to be under 35
    • They're also more likely to have attended grad school.
  • Audiobook listenership is most popular with adults aged 30 - 49.
    • This is followed closely by adults aged between 18 - 29.
  • Higher-income families are more likely than others to listen to audiobooks.

I also shared a poll on Instagram, simply asking who would be interested in a virtual book club.

Although my audience is predominantly tech enthusiasts and may not represent the general public, browsing genders, ages, and lifestyles of those who responded gave me useful demographic information.

Of the 88% who voted "yes," more than half were male, indicating interest among all genders, which previous research did not suggest.

These demographics led to the following personas, which will guide my design.
ALLISON, 17

  • High school student
  • Participates in and leads several extracurricular activities
  • Reads YA fiction in spare time


CAMERON-GEORGE, 26

  • Self-taught data scientist
  • Listens to several podcasts
  • Enjoys reading but does not always stay on top of it


EVE, 43

  • Pharmacist
  • Casual reader
  • Hosts a lot of gatherings at home
  • Deeply values education

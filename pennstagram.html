summary and goals
Instagram is one of today's largest photo- and video-sharing social networking applications. in my last semester as a master's candidate at the University of Pennsylvania (Penn), i created Pennstagram — a redesign of the popular app — based on user research.

user needs and research
to better understand what would make the best photo-sharing application, i surveyed 58 people. the following is a sample of the data collected.

research insights and follow-up phase
one takeaway was that users want more profile customizability, as indicated by the high number of people who wished to add some version of easily-installable profile themes. to accommodate this, i drafted the following design solutions:

multiple layout options for grid
header image
background color
option to show/hide likes and followers

i conducted a second phase of research with 32 participants, who were asked to provide feedback and rank the following grid styles based on the likelihood that they would use them on their profile: 2-columns, 3-columns, 4-columns, featured squares, and scrapbook style.

hover each artboard to view insights into the grid style.

n-column squares
featured squares
scrapbook

i concluded that there are a few main categories of users: those who like familiarity, prefer uniformity, or value dissimilarity within their grid. while it was great to hear from such a wide array, i believe giving the option of five grid styles could be overwhelming.

for this reason, i eliminated "featured squares" as a standalone option and opted to give users the choice to feature a post when creating it or viewing it later on. however, when a users edit their profiles, they are only given the options of classic squares or scrapbook. from there, they can choose between 1 and 4 columns, inclusive.

furthermore, during my first iteration of research, several participants noted difficulties navigating Instagram. during the second phase, i asked for specific interactions with the app that users found frustrating or confusing:

there's a camera in the corner, and i always tap it to make a new post, but it actually leads to the screen to create a new story.
whenever i want to see posts i've liked, i have to Google how to do it.
for context, it's under profile > settings > account > posts you've liked.

while i did not want to focus too much on restructuring Instagram's existing settings, i kept this user feedback in mind when adding more features and menus. i aimed to use clear, unambiguous icons and micro-interactions to give users feedback. additionally, when users edit their profiles, they see the impact of each modification in real-time rather than making edits and switching back to their profile to see the result.

final prototype and conclusions
redesigning a popular app was a fun project for my first full case study. below is a look at what i created as well as what i learned in the process.

in summary, photo-sharing apps attract creatives. as such, users want to feel involved with the look and feel of their profiles. rather than using apps like Instagram as a one-size-fits-all platform, many are beginning to use them as personal or business sites or mini-blogs — things that typically are highly customizable in their appearance.

however, giving users too many options to customize could make them feel overwhelmed, and it may be better to restructure the flow of options presented to the user. in this case, restructuring felt more logical and did not reduce the number of options!

with this, many prefer to use applications without needing external apps to complete simple tasks. an option to repost from others, keeping or modifying the original caption, was added to Pennstagram for this reason. it also gives users another way to interact with each other and feel more like "friends" than "followers."

further, users feel frustrated when navigation is not straightforward, when unlabeled icons are confusing, or when they have to alternate between screens to see the impact of their changes. thus, i made use of clear icons, micro-interactions, and real-time profile edits.

here is a recap of the features i integrated into Instagram, as seen in the demo:
- 24-hour "stories" display time remaining and a preview to help users decide if they want to view them
- repost from other users
- profile customizability:
    - a large, custom header image
    - a background color
    - two grid layout options
    - choice of 1-4 columns on profile grid
- feature a post upon creation or afterward
- control visibility of like-count on posts

just for fun: branding
although i am not a graphic designer, i enjoyed creating a logo and color scheme for pennstagram, both of which have meaning. the letters "nst" in the middle of the logo form a camera, and the animated version depicts the shutter being pressed. the gold-to-violet gradient used throughout Instagram was replaced with a red-to-blue gradient. red and blue are Penn's colors. go, Quakers!
